# Scout for the family

Scout is a little app that runs entirely on your own computer. The
**Markets** tab is the fun part: pick stocks you have a hunch about,
watch them live, get a notification if a move turns around, and trade
with $100,000 of practice money — real prices, zero risk. Nothing in
Scout can ever touch a real bank or brokerage account.

## If you got a Scout-for-Family.zip

Unzip it first: double-click the zip and you'll get a **Scout
Dashboard** folder. Put that folder somewhere permanent (Documents is
perfect), then follow your computer's section below. Setup happens once;
after that it's just the icon on your Desktop.

## Mac

1. Open the **Scout Dashboard** folder.
2. Open **Terminal**: press **Cmd+Space**, type `Terminal`, press Return.
   (One-time only — you'll never need it again.)
3. In the Terminal window, type `bash ` (the word and a space), then
   **drag the file `setup_mac.sh`** from the folder into the Terminal
   window, and press **Return**.
4. Wait a few minutes. If the Mac offers to install "command line
   developer tools", click **Install**, let it finish, and repeat step 3.
5. When it says **Done!**, there's a **Scout Dashboard** icon on your
   Desktop. Double-click it. The first time, the Mac may ask if you're
   sure — choose **Open**.

## Windows

1. Install Python if you don't have it: go to **python.org**, press the
   big yellow **Download** button, run the installer, and **tick the
   "Add python.exe to PATH" box** at the bottom before clicking Install.
2. Open the **Scout Dashboard** folder and double-click
   **Setup (Windows).bat**. Wait for "Done!".
3. Double-click the new **Scout Dashboard** icon on your Desktop.

## Using the Markets tab

- **Watch a ticker**: type a stock symbol (AAPL, TSLA, DIS…), say
  whether your hunch is *Going up* or *Going down*, press **Watch**.
  While the stock market is open (9:30–4:00 New York time, weekdays),
  Scout checks it every minute.
- **Tripwires**: if a stock starts moving against your hunch — crosses
  its average price the wrong way, gives back most of its gain, that
  kind of thing — a notification pops up on your screen and the alert
  appears in the feed.
- **Paper trading**: buy and sell with pretend money at real prices.
  The cards up top track how you're doing. Wipe it and start over any
  time under "Trade history & account".

Everything stays on your computer. Closing the window leaves Scout
running quietly in the background; restarting the computer stops it
(the Desktop icon brings it right back, watchlist and trades intact).
